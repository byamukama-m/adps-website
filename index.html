Our Story

About ADPS

Africa Development Program Services (ADPS) was created to respond to one of the biggest challenges in development work across Africa: strong ideas and funding often fail to translate into strong delivery.

Many organizations have ambitious missions, donor support, and committed teams — but struggle with program structuring, implementation systems, monitoring, and coordination across partners and countries.

ADPS exists to bridge this gap.

Founded by international development professionals with experience supporting complex, multi-stakeholder initiatives, ADPS helps institutional donors, governments, private philanthropists, corporations, and social enterprises to design, manage, strengthen, and scale development programs that deliver measurable impact.

Service Pillar Two

Program Audit & Impact Assurance

Independent verification and accountability

ADPS provides independent program audit and impact assurance services for donors who have disbursed funds directly to beneficiaries, NGOs, or partners and require objective verification.

Our audits go beyond financial checks to assess the full reality of program delivery and impact.

Impact assurance refers to ADPS's independent services that provide funders with confidence that:

  • resources were used as intended,
  • programs were implemented as agreed,
  • beneficiaries were genuinely reached, and reported results are credible and meaningful.

What We Audit

  • Use of funds & financial governance
  • Program delivery & partner performance
  • Field implementation & beneficiary reach
  • Outputs, outcomes & sustainability risks
  • Operational & reputational risks

Our Audit Approach

Each ADPS audit integrates:

  • Financial utilisation & governance review
  • Program delivery assessment
  • Field verification & beneficiary validation
  • Results, impact & sustainability analysis

Audits combine desk-based analysis with on-the-ground verification, resulting in clear, evidence-based reports and actionable recommendations.

  • Independent, professional verification
  • Field-based reality checks
  • Early identification of risks or misuse
  • Credible reporting for boards, families, and stakeholders
  • Stronger, informed funding decisions
